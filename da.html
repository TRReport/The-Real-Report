Democratic Alliance (DA)

What they said about Plastic City, and what really happened.

Official DA statements

After the demolition of Plastic City, DA ward councillor Brandon Pretorius told the press that “100% of the structures have been removed” and that “the real work begins now” with a 10‑point rehabilitation plan[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://www.citizen.co.za/brakpan-herald/news-headlines/2025/10/15/rehab-plan-in-motion-2/?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=1 "The Citizen").

“Plastic City cleared: Now the real work begins.” — Brakpan Herald, Oct 2025

Community reality

  • Families were left homeless with no relocation plan.
  • Illegal mining continues in nearby areas, untouched.
  • Residents say the DA claimed victory while ignoring the suffering on the ground.

Many locals feel the DA used Plastic City as a photo‑op and political credit grab, not as a real solution.

The Pretorius problem

Councillor Brandon Pretorius’s name has become tied to the demolition. For some residents, his statements felt like celebrating destruction rather than helping people. This has left a bitter taste — even those who share his surname feel embarrassed by the association.

Contradictions

The DA said the demolition was about safety and progress. But:

  • No clear relocation or housing was provided.
  • Police and metro officers were seen standing by while zama‑zamas carried on elsewhere.
  • The DA took credit, but the real drivers of the removal were metro police, SAPS, contractors, and local figures like Vlam.
